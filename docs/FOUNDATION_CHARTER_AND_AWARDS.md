## What the Foundation is

The Landscape Archive Foundation is the **public trust layer** of Landscape Archive — the place where **art × land × technology × data** meet as accountable public knowledge.

> The Landscape Archive Foundation holds the methods, evidence, and cultural forms that keep landscape knowledge accountable — across practice, art, computation, and the living world.

| | Product | Foundation |
|---|---|---|
| Role | **Instrument** | **Charter** |
| Examples | Archive, Hub, Studio+™, commercial packages | Open specification, field registry, crosswalks, awards, education, RFC governance |
| Job | Make work possible and deliverable | Keep methods, evidence, and cultural forms accountable to the commons |

Product ships tools. Foundation holds the trust layer those tools serve — and must not become Hub/Revit feature marketing dressed as charity.

## Four pillars (cultural frame)

Complementary to the published **five Foundation Standards** (botanical truth, climate disclosure, open infrastructure, public-sector interface, synthetic-nature ethics). This frame answers *why the Foundation exists culturally*; the Standards answer *what the open metadata steward publishes*.

| Pillar | Does | Does not |
|--------|------|----------|
| **Land** | Care for living systems, place, and practice | Generic nature content |
| **Art** | How we see and represent landscape | Decsheets on SaaS |
| **Technology** | Tools that make evidence usable | Hub / Revit feature marketing |
| **Data** | Traceable records, methods, openness for the commons | SEO dataset dumps |

## Awards brief

Awards belong in the Foundation when they:

1. Reward work at the **intersection** of the four pillars  
2. Create a **public, citable record** (not LinkedIn badges)  
3. **Feed the mission** — exemplars peers, universities, and agencies can inspect alongside the open specification  

Programme remains **in preparation** under interim draft governance (see live `/awards`). No entries or winners yet. Awards do not confer accreditation, registration, or vendor certification.

### Proposed strands (illustrative — founder may rename)

These strands are a **recognition lens**, not a replacement for the detailed category list already drafted on `/awards` (applied practice, data stewardship, craft, research, technology, climate, Indigenous storytelling, built-environment streams, etc.). Categories can map into strands; strands keep the programme honest about *why* something is recognised.

| Strand | Rewards |
|--------|---------|
| **Evidence** | Method, citation, climate/land accountability — claims that survive independent review |
| **Instrument** | Tool, workflow, and data craft in live practice — usable evidence, not demoware |
| **Form** | Art and representation that changes how landscape is seen — archival legibility, not spectacle alone |
| **Offset** | Fringe, care, uncommissioned, and sidelined practice — the “something off” slot |

### Offset — structural inclusion, not a silo

**Offset** is where the programme deliberately makes room for work the unmarked centre tends to miss: care labour, uncommissioned inquiry, fringe method, practices sidelined by prestige economies.

Gender-diverse and women practitioners often already work in this territory. Charter the strand and the jury so **inclusion is structural** — jury composition, nomination pathways, and assessment criteria that notice sidelined practice — **not** a pink-ribbon secondary category bolted beside unmarked “real” awards.

### What not to do

- Do **not** add a diversity checkbox category next to unmarked “real” awards (“Women in Landscape,” “Emerging Diverse Practitioner,” etc. as the only inclusion mechanism).  
- Do **not** treat awards as product marketing, vendor loyalty, or media-profile contests.  
- Do **not** open entries before published rubrics, conflict-of-interest rules, and assessment process are public.  
- Do **not** conflate Foundation recognition with commercial Archive / Hub entitlement.
