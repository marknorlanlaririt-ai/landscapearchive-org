# Draft consultation outreach — scripts & checklist

**Purpose:** Invite critique of the open TLA-185 schema pack / draft consultation — not product sales.  
**Tone:** Professional pride, vendor-neutral metadata, founding-alliance language **aspirational** (not promised seats).  
**Point people at:** Field Notes (*A shared vocabulary*), field registry / schema portal, Evidence Checker.  
**Do not mention:** Hub ops, Vault DAM, Workbench recipes, DocuSign machinery, pricing SKUs, proprietary pipelines.

**Public CTA:** https://landscapearchive.org/governance#draft-consultation  
**Request a review pack:** https://landscapearchive.org/contact#draft-consultation

---

## Email — technical director / BIM lead

```
Subject: Critique invite — open TLA-185 landscape metadata draft consultation

Dear [Name],

I’ve opened a draft consultation on a vendor-neutral landscape metadata specification (TLA-185) aimed at the infrastructure gap in digital handovers — planting and site-context claims that travel poorly between BIM, GIS, and asset registers.

Given your work on [project / practice digital delivery], I’d value your critique of the published schema pack before any founding-alliance model is finalised. This is not a product pitch and not an offer of membership seats — the Foundation is not yet incorporated; alliance language remains aspirational consultation only.

Useful starting points (open artefacts):
• Shared vocabulary Field Note: https://landscapearchive.org/articles/a-shared-vocabulary
• Field registry: https://landscapearchive.org/registry
• Schema portal: https://schema.landscapearchive.org
• Evidence Checker (inspect open TLA-185 JSON): https://landscapearchive.org/evidence-checker
• Request a review pack: https://landscapearchive.org/contact#draft-consultation

If you have 20–30 minutes for written notes — or prefer a short call — reply to this email. Mapping gaps you surface are intended as public RFC feedback, not a commercial conversation.

With thanks,
[Your name]
The Landscape Archive Foundation (interim stewardship)
```
