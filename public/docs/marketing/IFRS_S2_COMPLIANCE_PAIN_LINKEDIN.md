---

## Short (Foundation / founder voice)

```
Messy landscape metadata becomes sustainability-reporting risk when planting and site-context claims land in an appendix without joinable fields, evidence links, or method notes.

TLA-185 is open structure — a published field dictionary and crosswalks that map screening-grade site metadata to disclosure concepts teams already discuss (UK IFRS S2 / AASB S2 analogues). Supporting input for physical-risk and resilience narratives — not a substitute for entity GHG inventories, assurance, or filings.

## Medium

```
When landscape schedules live as opaque spreadsheets, sustainability teams inherit slogans (“native,” “resilient”) without taxon IDs, screening bands, or provenance — and the appendix becomes hard to defend under review.

The Landscape Archive Foundation publishes TLA-185 as open interchange grammar, plus concept crosswalks to UK IFRS S2 and AASB S2 climate-disclosure areas. These are supporting structures for decision support and procurement language — screening-grade, not statutory certificates, not GHG inventories, not “the only way” to report.

Crosswalks → https://landscapearchive.org/crosswalk/uk-ifrs-s2 · https://landscapearchive.org/crosswalk/aasb-s2
